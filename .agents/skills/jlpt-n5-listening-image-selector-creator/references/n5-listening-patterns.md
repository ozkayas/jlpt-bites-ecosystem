# JLPT N5 Listening (Mondai 1) Logic Patterns

To create authentic N5 listening questions, use one of these 6 primary distractor patterns found in real exams.

---

## 1. Reconsideration (Vazgecme / Change of Mind)
- **Concept:** Speakers discuss an option, agree on it, then one speaker raises a concern, leading to a final different choice.
- **Logic:** Option A -> Option B? -> Concern -> Final Option C.
- **Trap Design:** Show Option A (first mentioned), Option B (considered mid-dialogue), and Option C (final choice).
- **Example:**
  - "Let's buy a cake." -> "Is it too small?" -> "Yes, let's get cookies instead." -> "Actually, let's buy a sandwich."
  - Correct: Sandwich. Traps: Cake, Cookies, another food item.

## 2. Shortage (Eksik Tamamlama / Missing Item)
- **Concept:** Speakers list what they already have and identify what is missing. The question asks what they need to GET/BUY.
- **Logic:** Have A, Have B, No C -> Buy C.
- **Trap Design:** Show items they already have as distractors.
- **Example:**
  - "We have bread and juice. We only need butter."
  - Correct: Butter. Traps: Bread, Juice, Bread+Juice.

## 3. Attribute Filter (Nitelik Eleme / Feature Selection)
- **Concept:** Multiple similar objects are discussed, but only one fits all criteria (size, pattern, quantity, shape).
- **Logic:** Criteria 1 (Size: Small/Large) + Criteria 2 (Pattern: Fish/Flower) -> Only one matches both.
- **Trap Design:** Each distractor matches only one criterion, not both.
- **Important:** Since visuals are monochrome line art, do NOT use color as a distinguishing attribute. Use size, shape, pattern, quantity, or position instead.
- **Example:**
  - "The fish plate is nice but small. The flower one is large. Let's take the large one."
  - Correct: Large Flower Plate. Traps: Small Fish, Large Fish, Small Flower.

## 4. Negative Condition (Unutma/Hata / Forgetting/Mistake)
- **Concept:** A speaker mentions a condition that *should* be there but isn't, or an action that was *not* taken.
- **Logic:** "I forgot X" or "I didn't do Y" -> The image should show the *absence* of X.
- **Trap Design:** Show images with the item present as distractors.
- **Example:**
  - "It was sunny but I forgot my hat."
  - Correct: Person without hat in sun. Traps: Person with hat, person with umbrella, person with hat + umbrella.

## 5. Sequential Action (Sirasal Eylem / Order of Events)
- **Concept:** Speakers describe a sequence of events or actions. The question asks about the final state or what happens last.
- **Logic:** First A -> Then B -> Finally C -> Question asks about C (or the resulting state).
- **Trap Design:** Show intermediate states (after A, after B) and unrelated states as distractors.
- **Example:**
  - "First I went to the bank. Then I went to the post office. After that I ate lunch at the restaurant."
  - Question: "Where is the man now?" Correct: Restaurant. Traps: Bank, Post office, Home.

## 6. Location / Direction (Konum/Yon / Place or Direction)
- **Concept:** Speakers discuss where something is or which direction to go. The question asks to identify the correct location or path.
- **Logic:** Landmark A is [position] relative to B -> Find the matching layout.
- **Trap Design:** Mirror positions, swap landmarks, or change relative directions.
- **Example:**
  - "The bookstore is to the right of the station. The cafe is across from the bookstore."
  - Question: "Which map is correct?" Correct: Station-left, Bookstore-right, Cafe-across. Traps: Swapped positions.

---

## Pattern Selection Tips

| Pattern | Best For | Visual Delta (monochrome) |
|---------|----------|--------------------------|
| Reconsideration | Food, activities, plans | Different objects/activities |
| Shortage | Shopping, cooking, packing | Presence/absence of items |
| Attribute Filter | Clothing, objects, gifts | Size, pattern, shape, quantity (NO color) |
| Negative Condition | Weather, daily routines | Missing accessory/item |
| Sequential Action | Travel, daily schedule | Different locations/states |
| Location/Direction | Maps, room layouts | Spatial arrangement |
